==== Grading ====
At the end of a run, the player is given a grade in the form A of B (for example Five of Eight). Each number ranges from Zero to Ten, with the minimum grade being Zero of Zero and the maximum grade being Ten of Ten.

B is based purely on the player's progress. At every multiple of 111 levels (111, 222, 333, etc.), B will increment by one, eventually reaching Nine at level 999. If the credit roll is reached, B will become Ten if the credits are passed, and stay at Nine if the player tops out.

<!--- pre 1.7.0
<div class="toccolours mw-collapsible mw-collapsed" style="width:400px; overflow:auto;">
<div style="font-weight:bold;line-height:1.6;">'''A''' requirements (WARNING: SPOILERS FOR NORMAL MODE)</div>
<div class="mw-collapsible-content">

Similar to B, A has nine possible increments based on sections, and one separate requirement for the tenth.

* Every 100 levels is counted as a section for everything except the first 200 levels, which are combined into one section. In each of these sections, the player must score at least six tetrises for A to be incremented.

* If an all-clear is achieved during a section, the tetris requirement is reduced by two. This reduction can only occur once per section.

* If the player reaches the next section within a specific time limit, the tetris requirement is reduced by one. The required time varies depending on what section it is.

In practice this means that there are limited permutations of meeting a section requirement:
* 6 tetrises.
* 5 tetrises and beating the time requirement.
* 4 tetrises and getting an all-clear.
* 3 tetrises and getting an all-clear '''and''' beating the time requirement.
	

If all section requirements are met, A will only reach Nine. A is further incremented if the player reaches level 999 in 5:56 or less. Thus, A will only equal Ten if this time requirement is met, along with every section requirement.

The unfinished Kamui mode from ''[[TGM3]]'' is thought to be an early version of Asuka.

Clearing a [[Tetris]] or [[T-spin]] Triple (Standard only) gives you a Kita, while doing an [[All Clear]] gives a bigger Kita, which is displayed above the level. To advance to the next section, you need to have at least 1 Kita (that is, either clear a Tetris or TST or get an All Clear in the section). Any line clears that would pass a level stop without a Kita will not let you advance sections, and display Kita falling over.
	
If you do not have a Kita and achieve an All Clear at x99, you will still be unable to advance until the next line clear.

===== Rewind =====
During any time in the game, you can use the Rewind button to undo a previously placed piece, or a currently held piece. This can be done infinitely many times for a piece, and you can rewind multiple pieces at once. The rewind animation takes 61 frames to finish.  

You cannot rewind into the previous section, however.

===== Infinity =====
Unlike other modes in ''TGM4'', Asuka has [[infinity]], meaning there is no limit on how long you can stall a piece.

===== Vanish (Fading/Invisible Tetris) =====
Starting from level 1000, the game enters the Vanish phase, where pieces fade out from the stack some time after being placed. Any pieces that are placed before Level 1000 stay unaffected by this fading, and will stay visible until cleared.  

Starting from level 1200, all pieces placed become invisible immediately after being placed. 

Completing all 1300 levels of this mode awards the player with the ASUKA-MASTER (Am) grade.

===== Grading =====
When the game ends, the player is awarded a grade of Ae X, where X is a number between 1 and around 200, though there is technically no upper limit to the given grade.

X is calculated based on a variety of factors, including level reached, the number of Kitas obtained in each completed section, the number of Kitas obtained throughout the run, and the number of times Backstep was used.


The final value of X is calculated as follows:
* For each multiple of 70 levels reached, X is increased by 6.

* At level 1000, X is increased by an additional 7.

* At each section transition, X is increased by 3 for every 3 Kitas obtained within the completed section.

* A value equivalent to (Total Kitas obtained - Total Backsteps) / 2.5, rounded up, is added to X.

* If the final value of X is less than or equal to 0, no grade will be given.


In practice, this means the optimal strategy for Asuka Easy is to maximize the number of Kitas obtained while minimizing the use of Backstep, no matter the amount of time taken. This includes methods such as stacking up high at each level stop in order to allow for more tetrises in the following section. It is especially important to aim for multiples of 3 Kitas per section; for example: two completed sections with 6 Kitas each will award more grades than a section with 7 Kitas followed by a section with 5 Kitas.

Asuka Hard is a submode of Asuka that only unlocks once you obtained the ASUKA-MASTER title. It can be selected by pressing right while hovering over Asuka. The mode has a time limit of 4 minutes 30 seconds, with identical speed timings and Vanish phases to those of Asuka Normal. This submode can be played in practice via replays regardless of whether or not the player has achieved ASUKA-MASTER.

If you clear a section within a certain time limit, Kitas deducted will reduce by a certain amount.

====Pikii (300-399, 500-599) and Pikii-2 (700-999)====
At levels 300-399 and 500-599, the game will enter the Pikii section, where the bottom half of the playfield is frozen; any line clears in the frozen area will not clear until the section is passed, but they do count for levels as if they had cleared. The background of these "frozen" sections have their colors inverted.  

When a frozen section is passed, all finished line in the frozen area will be cleared, leaving only the unfinished lines.

At levels 700-999, the game switches to Pikii-2. The sections effectively works the same way as standard Pikii, but there are no unfrozen sections in-between. Upon a section pass, frozen lines will clear, but the bottom half will immediately re-freeze. Pikii-2 ends after level 999.

====Cyclone (1000+)====
Starting at level 1000 the game will enter the Cyclone section, which stays until the end of the game. Some pieces spawned will have their rotation randomized, as shown in the next box. Only the very next piece will show its rotation.

When a pre-rotated piece is held, it will retain its rotation preview. However, performing IRS and hold afterwards will preserve its post-IRS rotation.

Reaching level 1100 awards the player Master grade if the game does not reach the end.

====Master Pikii (1300+)====
Starting at level 1300, game enters the Master Pikii section, which stays until the end of the game. Each placed piece starting at Level 1300 will freeze after a certain time, including its separate minos if the piece is partially cleared. This time varies depending on how many medals you have obtained (see the table above). Pieces placed before Level 1300 stay unaffected.

The frozen minos do not clear when a line clear is made, but non-frozen minos in the line with them will clear as usual.

As a special gimmick, clearing a Tetris in these sections will also delete the bottom row of the playfield. This is the only way to remove frozen blocks.

====End game====

Master ends at Level 2600. If the player reaches level 2600 with a time greater than 7:15, [[torikan|the game will stop]], and the player is awarded the grade GRAND-MASTER (Gm).

Reaching level 2600 within 7:15 will cause the "END GAME" section to begin. During the "END GAME" section, the game will rewind all pieces placed in the last 65 seconds, putting you back at that point in time at the same level you had at that point, with the goal being to reach level 2600 within a 65 second time limit. The piece sequence will not be the same. Cyclone and Master Pikii are still active during this, but the playfield at the start of this section will be unfrozen. Medals will not be reversed either. There are no line clear requirements to get past every xx99 level. It is unknown if there is a line clear required to get past level 2599.

Reaching Level 2600 within the 65 second time limit will award the the player GRAND-MASTER! Rounds grade, while failing or topping out during END GAME will award the player the GRAND-MASTER (Gm) grade. An additional number may also be appended (for example Rounds-Six) depending on how far the game is rewound during "END GAME".

In practice mode, once 2600 is reached, a "Congratulations!" is given and the game ends; there is no end game.

In the game, Kita Kitsune will appear beside the playfield if the CPU detects an All Clear sequence is possible, celebrates when it's achieved or falls over when the opportunity is missed. It should be noted that it is not error-free and may miss some possible All Clears.
